Duke Kahanamoku

"Ambassador of Aloha"

Olympic Medalist and Avid Waterman

Helped Expose Surfing to The World

Duke Kahanamoku's Life

  • 1890 - Born in Honolulu, Hawaii
  • 1893 - Moved to Kālia, Waikiki
  • Developed his surfing and swimming skills at Waikiki Beach
  • Preferred a traditional surf board constructed after the fashion of ancient Hawaiian "olo" boards. Made from the wood of a koa tree, it was 16 feet (4.9 m) long and weighed 114 pounds (52 kg)
  • 1911 - Broke the world record in the 100 yards (91 m) freestyle by 4.6 seconds
  • 1912 - Won a gold medal in the 100-meter freestyle during the Summer Olympics in Stockholm
  • 1912 - Won a silver medal with the second-place U.S. team in the men's 4×200-meter freestyle relay during the Summer Olympics in Stockholm
  • 1920 - Won gold medals both in the 100 meters and in the relay during the Olympics in Antwerp
  • 1924 - Finished the 100 meters with a silver medal during the Olympics in Paris
  • 1932 - An alternate for the U.S. water polo team at the Summer Olympics
  • Gave surfing exhibitions during his international travels to swimming exhibitions, which popularized the sport of surfing
  • His surfing exhibition at Sydney's Freshwater Beach on December 24, 1914 is widely regarded as a seminal event in the development of surfing in Australia
  • 1925 - Rescued eight men from a fishing vessel that capsized in heavy surf while attempting to enter the city's harbor While living in Newport Beach, California
  • 1940 - Married Nadine Alexander
  • First person to be inducted into both the Swimming Hall of Fame and the Surfing Hall of Fame
  • The Duke Kahanamoku Invitational Surfing Championships are named in his honor
  • Served as sheriff of Honolulu, Hawaii from 1932 to 1961, serving 13 consecutive terms
  • Appeared in a number of television programs and films, such as Mister Roberts (1955)
  • 1968 - Died of a heart attack at the age of 77
  • 2002 - On the 112th anniversary of his birth, a 37-cent first-class letter rate postage stamp of the United States Postal Service with Duke's picture on it, was issued
  • 2015 - A monument featuring a replica of Kahanamoku's surfboard was unveiled at New Brighton beach, Christchurch, New Zealand in honour of the 100th anniversary of Kahanamoku's visit to New Brighton
  • 2015 - A Google Doodle honored the 125th anniversary of Duke Kahanamoku's birthday
